Use cases

The overall principles of automatic data acquisition, analysis and control can be applied to various application domains. Data collection is not limited to IoT sensors but can be imported from user surveys, inspections or corporate IT systems.

  1. Anybody, and you yourself, can benefit from a system where they can store information about their assets, such as purchase information, photos, manuals and invoices. In addition, it can provide a central place to store checklists, procedures, instructions and recipies. It also provides an environment to track and monitor your own health, such as weight, heart rate, blood pressure and vision. This stays on you own private server, not somewhere in the cloud.

  2. Home owners have different apps for controlling lighting, heating, entertainment systems, lawn mower, garden irrigation and weather forecast. They benefit from a single app where they can view all essential information on a single screen without having to switch between apps and having to learn and install new apps. They also may want to setup automations and monitoring that work across all these applications.

  3. Managers need to set targets for their employees that are specific and measurable over time. They can define key performance indicators such as sales targets, help desk response time or number of incidents. They are forced to define a procedure on where this information comes from and how it is collected, either automatically or manually via a form. A smart contract between the manager and the employee automatically checks compliance of the actuals versus target and issues notifications in case of discrepancies.

  4. Building operators need real-time and historical information about space occupancy to improve the well being of our occupants and save money on cleaning. For example, if certain areas are high in demand I want to create more of those. Also, if nobody has used the conference rooms in the last 24hr there is no need for cleaning.

  5. Fleet operators need real-time information on vehicle location and usage statistics to that they can optimize the dispatch and ensure driver compliance. For example, they want to see which car has no passengers and is close to a client pickup and monitor the maximum speed and G-forces to ensure a good client experience.

  6. Human resource managers want to analyze the common characteristics of employees with good reviews and low turnover so that they can improve pre-screening of new candidates. For example, if 80% of the people with main hobby 'watching movies' leave within 1, year they want this answer to raise a red flag on an job candidate screening survey.
